Designers are not always aware of the impact they can have on people who come in contact with their work. Unethical choices can result in harmful experiences. The Designer’s Oath provides a guideline for designers on ethical design. Sign the Oath and spread the word.

Code of ethics for designers

I SWEAR THAT

I WILL RESPECT THE USER’S NEEDS

  • I will put the interest of the user first and i will use my voice to pleat for their interests.
  • My design should respect the user and their context.

I WILL SHARE MY KNOWLEDGE

  • I will help the world with my work.
  • I will promote my profession and contribute knowledge and skills within my occupation.

MY DESIGN WILL BE INCLUSIVE

  • Design must be inclusive; do not exclude anybody.
  • The designer must know for whom he is designing for.

I WILL PRESENT MY WORK AS TESTABLE, AND THEREFORE WILL BE TRANSPARENT.

I WILL TRY TO UNDERSTAND THE STAKEHOLDERS, RE-EVALUATE THEIR IDEAS, KEEP THEM UP TO DATE AND BE HONEST TO THEM

I WILL DO MY BEST TO DO NO HARM TO ANY USER AND RESPECT THEIR PRIVACY

I WILL WELCOME CRITICISM AND FEEDBACK

  • I will watch my language of trade-offs
  • I will be considerate of my peers.
  • I will evaluate my work for ethics along the way.

I WILL TAKE RESPONSIBILITY FOR MY ACTIONS

  • I will not abuse my knowledge.
  • I will evaluate design on the value of the impact and respect the environment in my design
  • I shall give credit to the resources I used

I swear to fulfil, to the best of my ability and judgement, and that I will keep my word and stand by this oath. This way, I will maintain the integrity of the designer.
